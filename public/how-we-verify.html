How We Verify

Transparency and safety are at the core of everything we do

Official Data Sources

All medicine information on our platform is sourced directly from official government drug regulatory databases and approved pharmaceutical registries. We cross-reference multiple authoritative sources to ensure accuracy.

  • Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) database
  • National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM)
  • WHO International Nonproprietary Names (INN)

Manufacturer Verification

We maintain strict standards for the manufacturers listed on our platform. Every manufacturer must meet internationally recognized quality and safety certifications.

  • WHO Good Manufacturing Practices (WHO-GMP) certification
  • ISO 9001:2015 quality management certification
  • FDA approvals and compliance (where applicable)
  • Regular audits and compliance checks

Safety & Efficacy of Generic Medicines

Generic medicines contain the same active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) as their branded counterparts and are equally safe and effective. Here's what you need to know:

Same Active Ingredient: Generic medicines have the exact same chemical composition and strength as branded versions.

Bioequivalence: They must demonstrate bioequivalence, meaning they work in the body the same way as the original medicine.

Regulatory Approval: All generic medicines require approval from drug regulatory authorities before they can be sold.

Cost Savings: Lower prices are due to reduced marketing and research costs, not inferior quality.
