Yes. Generic medicines are approved by regulatory authorities (like the FDA) and must have the same active ingredient, strength, dosage form, and route of administration as the brand-name drug. They are bioequivalent, meaning they work in the body in the same way.

Generic drug manufacturers do not have to repeat the expensive clinical trials that the brand-name company already conducted. They also spend less on advertising and marketing. These cost savings are passed on to you, the consumer.

Simply ask! You can say, "Is there a generic alternative for this medicine?" or "Can my prescription be filled with a generic?" Your doctor or pharmacist can advise if a generic equivalent is available and appropriate for you.
